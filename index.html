    Frequently Asked Questions

    1. How can dyslexia be treated or supported?

    Structured literacy programs (e.g., Orton-Gillingham) Multisensory learning techniques Assistive technology (e.g., text-to-speech software) Extra time on tests and accommodations in school/work

    2. Is dyslexia only about reading and writing?

    No, it can also affect memory, organization, sequencing, and sometimes math skills (dyscalculia).

    3. Are people with dyslexia less intelligent?

    No, dyslexia does not affect intelligence. Many people with dyslexia are highly creative and excel in problem-solving, arts, and entrepreneurship.

    4. Can adults have dyslexia?

    Yes, dyslexia is lifelong. Many adults develop coping strategies, but they may still struggle with reading, writing, or organization.

    5. What careers can people with dyslexia succeed in?

    People with dyslexia can thrive in many careers, especially those that emphasize creativity, problem-solving, and hands-on skills. Common fields include entrepreneurship, arts and design, engineering, technology, sports, and public speaking. Many successful individuals with dyslexia have found innovative ways to work around their challenges and use their strengths to excel.
